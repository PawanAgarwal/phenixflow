Because a 177 000 payroll increase is 25% above the 12-month average of 152 000, and wage growth at 3.8% y/y underscores broad hiring and pay momentum. Meaning that, even amid policy shocks, firms continue “hoarding labor,” a hallmark of a strong economy. Despite market pressure for cuts, Powell has repeatedly emphasized data dependence and the Fed’s higher-for-longer bias, warning that pre-emptive easing could reignite inflation . By beating jobs forecasts, April’s report further consolidates his authority to maintain rates until clear slack emerges, underscoring that markets must dance to Powell’s tune.   CME Fed‐funds futures show June cut odds plummeting, with traders now pushing the first cut out to late 2025.    However most of April’s hiring decisions occurred under pre‐tariff conditions, meaning the 177 000 payroll gain overstates underlying strength once tariff‐driven headwinds fully materialize.
