Let me try to answer the questions.
1) the relationship between volatility and liquidity is inverse. High volatility typically means low liquidity, and vice versa. This is because when liquidity is high,  bidders can easily meet sellers, leading to narrower spreads and lower volatility. On the other hand, when liquidity is low, bidders may struggle to find sellers, resulting in wider spreads and higher volatility.
2) a negative speed profile means shorter gamma on the upside and longer gamma on the downside. When spot moves up, customers are bidding which makes MM shorter delta. To stay delta-neutral, MM has to buy futures or options, as a result, more bidding and more demand increases volatility. Conversely, if spot moves down, the longer gamma forces MM to sell futures or options to maintain delta-neutral. the force of sellers increases and bids are easily filled, resulting higher liquidity and lower volatility.
