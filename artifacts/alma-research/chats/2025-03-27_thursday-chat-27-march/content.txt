I'll explain the options Greeks concepts in Alma's post and answer the specific questions about vomma and zomma.
Understanding the Key Concepts
Alma is describing how market maker positioning changes based on where the S&P 500 (SPX) is trading relative to a specific put strike (5565), and how volatility changes affect their hedging needs.
Long vs Short Vomma
How is long/short vomma determined here?
Vomma (or volga) measures how vega changes with respect to volatility. In the context of this post:
Long vomma position: Above the put strike (5565), market makers have long vomma exposure. This means their vega exposure becomes more positive as volatility increases.
Short vomma position: Below the put strike (5565), market makers have short vomma exposure. This means their vega exposure becomes more negative as volatility increases.
The positioning is determined by the overall options structure market makers have on their books around this significant put strike level.
Vomma's Effect on Zomma
How does long/short vomma exposure affect zomma increasing/decreasing?
Zomma measures how gamma changes with respect to volatility. In Alma's explanation:
When market makers are long vomma (above strike), decreasing volatility leads to increasing positive zomma until ~5858-5870 range. This accelerates delta changes, requiring them to buy futures when hedging.
When market makers are short vomma (below strike), decreasing volatility leads to increasing positive zomma until ~5110-5115 range. This also accelerates delta changes, but requires them to sell futures when hedging.
The relationship works because both vomma and zomma are higher-order derivatives related to volatility. When volatility decreases, the rate of change in hedging needs (zomma) increases in the direction of the vomma exposure.
Practical Trading Implications
What Alma is describing is important for understanding potential market moves. As volatility decreases:
If SPX is above 5565: Market makers need to buy more futures as prices rise (potentially accelerating upward moves)
If SPX is below 5565: Market makers need to sell more futures as prices fall (potentially accelerating downward moves)
This creates a "convexity" effect that can amplify market movements in either direction as volatility falls, with specific target ranges where these effects might be strongest.

//@version=5
indicator("GKYZ Volatility", shorttitle="GKYZ Vol. Est.", overlay=false)

lookback = input.int(5, title="Lookback Period", minval=1)  
ma_period=input.int(20,title="MA Period", minval=1)

f_calcDailyRV() =>

    window          = lookback
    trading_periods = 390

    log_hoD = math.log(high / open)
    log_loD = math.log(low  / open)
    log_coD = math.log(close / open)

    log_ocD    = math.log(open / nz(close[1]))
    log_oc_sqD = log_ocD * log_ocD

    log_ccD    = math.log(close / nz(close[1]))
    log_cc_sqD = log_ccD * log_ccD

    rsD = log_hoD*(log_hoD - log_coD) + log_loD*(log_loD - log_coD)

    close_volD = math.sum(log_cc_sqD, window) / (window - 1)
    open_volD  = math.sum(log_oc_sqD, window) / (window - 1)
    window_rsD = math.sum(rsD, window)        / (window - 1)
    kD         = 0.34 / (1.34 + (window + 1.0)/(window - 1.0))
    rvD        = math.sqrt((open_volD + kD*close_volD + (1 - kD)*window_rsD) * trading_periods)

dailyRV = request.security(syminfo.tickerid, "1", f_calcDailyRV())
ma=ta.sma(dailyRV,ma_period)
plot(dailyRV, title="GKYZ Volatility", color=#970ef3, linewidth=2)
plot(ma,title="Moving Average", color = #00ff00, linewidth=2)

2025-03-27T20:49:12.968Z | Rooster
You have to read the gamma and charm maps together, recognizing they reflect dealer mechanics and reaction to price action only, and are not deterministic.  So the orange hues reflect negative gamma, meaning the dealer mechanics will increase the magnitude of the move, up or down.  (In negative gamma, if spot moves up, dealers' delta goes down, so they have to buy more deltas (futures) to stay delta neutral.  If spot goes down, dealers' delta goes up, so they have to sell deltas to stay delta neutral).  The blue on the charm map is negative charm, that is, dealers' delta decays with time, meaning they have to buy deltas to stay delta neutral and that acts as a bit of a tailwind/supportive.  Yellow on the charm map is positive charm, meaning dealers' delta increases with time and they have to sell deltas as time passes and that acts as a headwind/suppressive. So we saw a nice rally assisted by negative gamma despite the suppressive charm and almost made it into the supportive charm when selling interest came in, and then that negative gamma accentuated the down move (dealers selling with the seller).  And this next part is beyond my current understanding but the very faintly shaded area on the gamma map indicates low speed (very little change in gamma with spot) which (I believe) allows volatility to play a more dominant role and we saw RV jump the last 20 min or so.
