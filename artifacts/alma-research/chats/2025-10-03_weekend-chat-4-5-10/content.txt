1) the best meassure of skew is the 25d put minus 25d call, normalized to ATM vol. it is basically the slope, yes
I use 22 deltas.
similarly with the tails.
use fix strike skew and monitor the difference in %
(I purposely don't use 'skew sold' or 'skew bought' expressions, bcs many ppl would be confused again... because sold skew = shorter skew = put IV down, bough skew = longer skew = call IV down)

2) first you need this https://datashop.cboe.com/cboe-options-open-close-volume-summary
, but you should contant them, and they will tailor your request to your needs and possibilities..

3) it is the "tail wags the dog" effect, bcs volatility can be traded, and so the wall street can directly influence the vol of vol.
what is VIX? the IV of the next 30 days SPX options. so when you trade with VIX options, you trade with the vanna and vomma of the next 30 days SPX options, bcs options trading is volatility trading too, and as a result of supply/demand + dealer flows the convexities or concavities can be artificially flattened or steepened.
So, for example if an MM has long vanna and long vomma exposure, whats gonna happen with the curve if vol goes up? the vega almost-bell curve will elevate at the 10-20 deltas, consequently the vanna curve will flatten (bcs vanna = vega to spot), so the more vol goes up, the less delta the MM will get to hedge, and as a result the realized log returns will start to lessen relatively.
or from the perspective of vega, the more spot goes down (long vanna environment) when vol catched up, the less his vega exposure will change = less and less additional vega to hedge leading to less vol bid.
this is why vomma handles the realized kurtosis of the distribution.

4) charm matters around the expo itself... that passive selling is not too exciting to be honest. after a lot of backtest, betting on charm is worse than a coinflip. no edge.
color is more important in my read.

5) many ppl here recognized that the premarket OD heatmap (the one without the 0DTE updates) is more accurate than the 10 min updated one. Do you know why? bcs the traders trade the structure!
and on the not-updated OD chart you see exactly the structure that the traders are betting on. the 0DTE update is mostly noise, positioning shifts very fast.
this is why a good volume profile is also enough for intraday trading. (no vol trading then tho)
knowing how to read the sentiment, how to derive the structure and the distribution + knowing about delta neutral and also momentum strategies is what really matters.
Positioning is a good source of info... i can derive the distribution from it, including volatility. better edge. but the overemphatize of dealer flow is just a salesman strategy for many, like if it would be the 'hidden force' that drives the markets... no free lunch, market is stochastic. and dealer flow is a double negative: i buy a call - dealer short the call (short delta) - dealer buys futs to hedge
