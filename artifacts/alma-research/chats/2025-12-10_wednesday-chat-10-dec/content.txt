2025-12-10T22:42:10.817Z | Alma
realized volatility is literally nothing but the standard deviation of the logreturns (over a certain period)
I have a full post on it.

2025-12-10T22:44:03.728Z | Alma
liquidity is something like volume, it tells how rich the order book is.
officially: “Liquidity generally refers to how easily or quickly a security can be bought or sold in a secondary market.”

2025-12-10T22:44:40.795Z | Alma
if liquidity is high, the market is closer to be efficient,
and volatility is low, bcs buyers and sellers are agreed

2025-12-10T22:45:49.678Z | Alma
there are liquid and illiquid zones in the orderbook.
illiquid zones are jumbed through fast,
vol increases there, price spends little time there.

while at liquid zones, spot tends to hover more, and does not jump big, less or no gaps in the orderbook, so the returns are also compressed, hence volatility is lower

2025-12-10T22:46:05.714Z | Alma
so high liquidity = low RV
low liquidity = high RV

2025-12-10T22:46:56.387Z | Alma
also I said this, but i reiterate, that realized volatility is basically the velocity of the price changes, bcs SD of returns is a series in time

2025-12-10T22:49:24.571Z | Alma
hence: low vol at a level means spot tends to spend more time there.
and this says that the frequency of the more liquid spot area will be higher than the less liquid/illiquid ones,
hence when you plot the daily changes in a histogram where x-axis is the spot levels, and y-axis is the frequency, you will get the distribution.

where vol was low, the distribution will give you a higher value, compared to zones where vol was lower.

2025-12-10T22:50:49.600Z | Alma
so the incidenc rate compared to the whole will be higher for low vol/high liquidity zones,
and smaller for high vol/lower liquidity zones.
meaning: the probability will be higher for low vol zone,
and lower for higher vol zones
