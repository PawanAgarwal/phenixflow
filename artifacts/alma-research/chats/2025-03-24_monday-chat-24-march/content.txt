think like this:
delta = premium to spot
vanna = (premium to spot) to vol = delta to vol

gamma = (premium to spot) to spot = delta to spot
zomma = [(premium to spot) to spot] to vol = (delta to spot) to vol = gamma to vol

but it can also be written as
[(premium to spot) to vol] to spot = (delta to vol) to spot = vanna to spot

...assuming the option pricing function is smooth

2025-03-24T10:10:06.402Z | Leonida
1) It could be that it depends on whether they are above or below the put’s strike, during the last two hours, which essentially acts as 0 Vanna . If they are above, they might opt for a long OTM call since it has long Vanna and the market makers become shorter Vega, thus increasing volatility. In other words, they choose an OTM call that has long Vanna (higher prob it can go ITM take advantage of the upside move, as stated in the brochure “investors may forgo some upside”). If they are below, they do the opposite: they would sell OTM calls because they are short Vanna and the market makers become longer Vega, thereby decreasing volatility.

Both calls would hedge the delta but with different effects based on Vanna. (I thought like this, hope it makes sense)
