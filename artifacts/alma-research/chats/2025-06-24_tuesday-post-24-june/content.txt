When time passes or IV falls, the distribution curve gets smaller. OTM options (the majority are OTM) lose deltas. Dealers rebalance their hedges on this (buy back short deltas). Since the world is long (Sell call/buy put), this forces the market higher after events, that raises IV. Of course there are outliers. But generally speaking, after big events, where the investors are well hedged, the market goes higher afterwards. This makes shorting difficult and being long easy.

As a result, dealers accumulate increasing short call exposure (negative gamma), forcing them to raise IV as spot moves higher, creating a “spot up, vol up” environment.
As IV rises, gamma decreases, which makes dealer hedging less effective. This breakdown in hedging dynamics can cause a decoupling, or "unpinning" of spot and volatility, accelerating the move and resulting in a true gamma squeeze.
