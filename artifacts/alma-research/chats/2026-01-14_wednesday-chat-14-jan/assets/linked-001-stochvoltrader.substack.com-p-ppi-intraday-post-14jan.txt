PPI   ’was made’ dovish on near-term inflation momentum (core m/m = 0 vs +0.2) but hawkish on the 12‑month inflation backdrop (y/y = 3% vs 2.7).   Most desks react fastest to core m/m because it’s “current momentum” and feeds forward inflation expectations.   If yields fall and Nasdaq leads that means, market is weighting core m/m. If yields rise back and Dow/value holds better that means market is rotating toward “y/y stickiness” framing.    Inflation impulse (near-term) is cooling, but inflation backdrop (12‑mo) is sticky. Momentum remains dovish (CPI core cooler vs exp; PPI core m/m even cooler vs exp), but the narrative gets noisier.   Choppiness, and and more rotational than smooth trend is expected further.
