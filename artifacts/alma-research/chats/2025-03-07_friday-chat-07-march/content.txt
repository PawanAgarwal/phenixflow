yesterday you asked me "Do you ever choose to short the overpriced trade instead of longing the underpriced trade?"
I type my answer here instead:

2025-03-07T09:11:40.526Z | Alma
i go for overpriced vol.. if know what i mean... so only bcs a trade is crowded doesn't mean it can't print.
the idea here is that I know that this is an oscillating market with a negative drift, so i know that there will be upward move, and bcs it is short speed, MMs are shorter upside vol = more edge on playing calls.
but as you could see, the spx/vix beta went up on that move, so the whole smirk was bid, including tails, meaning if i would have sold puts instead, i wouldn't have printed.
contraintuitively vol selling is better when i see that upward move is done, and market is heading down so spot down/vol down, and you can see that spx/vix beta again spiked on those downward moves, meaning the smirk was hammered. negative speed environment.

but the main thing is to get out fast. vol trade can be slow and vol of vol is still sensitive. I do make short vol positions during the day, I just don't post those (too complicated for some plp and I don't want to confuse them before I dropped the educationals on them), but i estimate the best sortino on those call side long vol trades.

Btw, I like to use Heston-Nandi type of Monte Carlo simulation to get a distribution for the possible vol paths (i posted a pic of that in one of my intraday posts), and thats what i use as reference to see if vol is actually over/underpriced or not.
this is crucial, bcs if something is expensive doesn't necessary mean it is overpriced, and vice versa.
