It unanimously voted to keep the federal funds rate at 4.25–4.50%, with the updated projections still showing a median of two rate cuts in 2025 despite higher inflation forecasts and geopolitical uncertainty around tariffs and the Middle East. Powell’s post-meeting comments balanced reassurance on inflation’s gradual cooling with caution over trade-driven price pressures, signaling the Fed remains data-dependent but in no hurry to ease further.   Each $10 rise in oil (Hormuz threats) adds ~0.3 % to CPI, reviving stagflation fears. Geopolitical noise outweighs expectations of Fed easing. Only a significant disinflation in core CPI and wage growth would bring cuts as soon as September—otherwise, the Fed remains on hold into 2026. Of 19 policymakers, 8 forecast two 25 bp cuts in 2025, while 7 expect no cuts, making the median of two cuts a fragile compromise. US bond market is about to crack.
