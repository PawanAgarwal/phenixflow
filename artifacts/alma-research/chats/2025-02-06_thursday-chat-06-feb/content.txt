2025-02-06T07:39:46.410Z | Alma
@Jeffrey K for your yesterday question.
schematically, speaking:
think about gamma as the amount dealers have to trade when spot moves by one unit.
if you are long lets say 10 gamma,
if spot moves up, you have to sell 10 shares of stocks to adjust your initial hedge (doesn't matter if you hedge call or put, the ending result is the same)

okay, now we now that gamma changes relative to spot (called "speed").
so lets say one unit higher you only long 8 gamma
means, if spot moves up, you will still sell against the price move, but only 8 shares of stocks this time.

on the next lvl, you only long 5 gamma
again, if spot moves up, you short sell only 5 shares of stocks. and so on...
so price has less and less resistance from gamma based delta hedging, and it lets the range of returns expand slowly to the upside, causing RV to expand a bit.

now, if it reaches zero gamma line, then there is no gamma impact on that lvl when spot moves up or down, but then it flips to negative.
And when you have negative gamma, lets say you short 5 gamma, if spot moves up, you have to BUY 5 more shares of stock. buying this time.

quick math:
if you are long ATM put: you have -50 delta, you long 50 shares of stock.
in positive gamma exposure, lets say you long 2 gamma, if spot moves up by one unit, you will have -50 + 2 = -48 delta to hedge, you only need to hedge 48 delta, so you sell 2 shares of stock
now, in negative gamma exposure, if you would have short that 2 gamma, if spot moves up by one unit, you would have -50 + -2 = -52, so you would have buy 2 more shares of stocks.

if you are long ATM call: you long 50 delta, you short 50 shares of stock,
long 2 gamma, spot up: 50 + 2 = 52, sell 2 shares of stock
short 2 gamma, spot up: 50 -2 = 48, buy back 2 shares of stock.

same thing happens with calls and puts

Now this is a good question.

The premium is different. ITMs are more expensive.
OTMs are very cheap.

For intraday 0DTE trading I prefer ITMs mostly.
Why? Bcs the premium you pay for it includes intrinsic value that doesn't erode over time.
ATMs and OTMs only have extrinsic value that decay away by the time.
Very important.
I use ATM leg for 0DTE only if I know that I will hit it fast and close it fast, bcs ATM has the highest gamma.

If I want to take smaller position however, I prefer taking 1-2DTE, 25 delta contract.
Never 0DTE, bcs of fast time decay.
It is cheap.

For longer-term trades like weekly monthly, I prefer 22-25 delta OTM strikes due to the gamma convexity,
and when I see an oppotunity, or a downstick to buy into it but closer to the original leg's expo, I will add a 40-50 delta near-the-money or ATM leg too.

This is it
