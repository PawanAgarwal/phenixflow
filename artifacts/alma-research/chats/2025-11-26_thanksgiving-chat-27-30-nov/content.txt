2025-11-30T17:30:49.613Z | Alma
speed and vanna have usually the same net exposures, but not always. for vanna you take multiple expos, where vol responses differ.
vanna is also a bridge between implied and realized volatility.
but it is more like a mirror of the reflexive effects:
like for example:

traders net sell upside calls and buy downside puts = long skew.
dealers will sell the skew, bcs they buy those upside calls and sell those downside puts.
the result is a net positive dealer vanna profile.

what customers are doing here is bidding up the upside gammas, or in otherwords: selling upside realized volatility.

now of course, their gamma/RV selling becomes profitable only if the spot is grinding higher.
so if they are kind of "confident" with their bet, the customer flow will be a net seller of IV.
And if IV goes down, delta goes down as well, as the profile is dealer long vanna. The key is to see, in which tenor the selling occurs the most (fixed strike skew analysis on diff tenors).
so this is a supportive flow that creates a constant passive buying flow by the dealers, and will make the spot to grind upward.
