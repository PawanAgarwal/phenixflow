From ZoomyZoom and Claude interpretation - The AI Money Flow Problem:
What's happening:
You pay $200 → Cursor gets $200
Cursor pays $500 to OpenAI (losing $300, covered by VC money)
OpenAI pays $1000 to AWS (losing $500, covered by VC money)
AWS pays $10,000 to NVIDIA for chips
The "House of Cards" Problem:
Everyone's losing money except NVIDIA - Only the chip maker profits while everyone else burns cash
VC subsidies everywhere - The whole chain only works because venture capitalists are funding the losses at every level
Upside-down economics - Each company pays MORE for their input costs than they collect from customers
NVIDIA captures all the value - They're the only ones with pricing power and real profits. The AI industry works the same way - it's entirely dependent on VC funding to subsidize losses at every level. When that funding dries up, companies either need to:
Raise prices dramatically (killing demand)
Cut costs drastically (hurting quality)
Go out of business
That's why it's a "house of cards" - remove the VC subsidy, and the whole structure collapses.
