 The YLD family of funds sells covered calls on a range of underlying indices, including the Nasdaq 100 (NDX), S&P 500, and Russell 2000. While that broad selling impacts trading into Friday’s expiration, as billions in notional delta get sold across major indices, today’s focus is on QYLD — which specifically targets the NDX.

QYLD systematically sells billions of dollars in at-the-money NDX calls for the following month’s AM expiration. These positions are typically bought back the Thursday before expiration — which is today — as part of their roll process.

According to their strategy, QYLD executes these buybacks throughout the day at the volume-weighted average price (VWAP). Although no specific time is disclosed, I’ve noticed that volume tends to pick up significantly between 1:00 and 2:00 PM ET during this window. That flow often impacts index movement, providing a supportive or bullish push as negative delta is bought back and positive delta is added into the market.
