SPX 0DTE Asymmetrical Butterfly Calculation
You have an asymmetrical put butterfly spread with strikes at 5995/6005/6015 for SPX expiring today (Feb 7, 2025). Let’s break down the cost basis, breakeven points, and risk profile.
1. Position Breakdown
+6 5995 Puts @ 2.90
-9 6005 Puts @ 4.5133
+3 6015 Puts @ 7.84
2. Net Cost Calculation (Basis per spread)
Each spread consists of:
6 Long 5995 Puts @ 2.90 → Total Cost = 6 × 2.90 = 17.40
9 Short 6005 Puts @ 4.5133 → Total Credit = 9 × 4.5133 = 40.62
3 Long 6015 Puts @ 7.84 → Total Cost = 3 × 7.84 = 23.52
Net Cost = (Long 5995 Cost + Long 6015 Cost) - (Short 6005 Credit)
= (17.40 + 23.52) - 40.62
= 0.30 debit per spread
Total cash outlay (assuming each contract represents 100 SPX):
= 0.30 × 100 = $30 per spread
3. Breakeven (B/E) Points
Since this is a butterfly structure, your breakeven points are derived from the spread width and net debit:
Upper B/E: 6015 - Net Debit = 6015 - 0.30 = 6014.70
Lower B/E: 5995 + Net Debit = 5995 + 0.30 = 5995.30
4. Risk & Reward Profile
Max Risk: The debit paid ($30 per spread).
Max Profit: Achieved if SPX closes at 6005, where the short leg (6005 put) is ITM while long legs (5995 and 6015) are balanced.
Max Profit = (Width of strikes - Net Debit) × 100
= (10 - 0.30) × 100 = $970 per spread
Max Loss: Limited to the $30 per spread paid.
Profit Zone: Between 5995.30 and 6014.70.
5. Summary of Trade
Cost Basis: $30 per spread
Breakeven: 5995.30 – 6014.70
Max Profit: $970 per spread (if SPX closes at 6005)
Max Loss: $30 per spread
Risk-Reward Ratio: 1:32 (extremely favorable if SPX pins 6005)
Final Thoughts
