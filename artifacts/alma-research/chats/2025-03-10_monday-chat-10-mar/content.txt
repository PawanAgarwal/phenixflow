Short Volatility Position:
 selling a 22 delta call (short call) with an expiration in March. A 22 delta call means the call option has about a 22% chance of ending in the money by expiration, and you’re expecting volatility to decrease or remain stable. By selling this call, you collect premium, betting that the price won’t rise too much.

Long Put Leg:
You also have a long put. This means you’ve bought a put option. A put option profits when the underlying asset decreases in price. By having a long put, you’re protecting yourself if the market goes down significantly, giving you some downside protection.

Intraday Reversion:
You’re expecting a price reversion during the day, meaning you anticipate the price to swing back after an initial move. You could be looking for short-term price stability or a small move, so you're aiming to profit from the intraday volatility.
