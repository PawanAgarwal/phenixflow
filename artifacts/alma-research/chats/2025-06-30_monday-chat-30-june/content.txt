2025-07-01T06:56:36.202Z | Ricardo
I am long OTM calls from July 31 and short futures against it, making me essentially long gamma. If the market moves up, the calls profit and the futures make a loss. If the market goes down, the futures make a profit and the calls lose. Since I am positive gamma, the rate of change is favorable for my position. I lose, if the market does not move at all.
I am not exposed to directional movements in any way (delta) and protected against crazy moves, or headlines, but I pay every day theta to hold that position. My profits are made with realized vol (gamma) being higher then expected vol (IV).
To protect the calls from losing value in a drop in vol, I bought the expiration after the Fed date. Since the market is nervous about decisions from the Fed, vol stays bid or even rizes behind that date.
Since we are in a spot up / vol up regime and vol hit a floor, the movement in vol is favorable for long options.
If spot goes up and vol too, OTM calls make profits on both. As one leg is a future, I can close and open that leg as I like. If we hit a daily support, I can close the future (losses from the calls were reduced) and let the calls profit from the recovery. I short the future again, when a daily resistance is hit and lock in the gains from the calls (as the futures will make a profit, if the market falls).
