A risk reversal compares demand for puts vs calls.
Short risk reversal = puts are bid relative to calls
Market participants are paying more for downside protection
In practical terms:
Dealers tend to be short puts / long calls
That positioning often creates supportive flows on dips
So the market structure is usually:
Downside moves get bought
Upside can grind or squeeze
It’s not outright bearish positioning.It often behaves like a dip-buy environment unless key levels break.
2. The Key Level: 6785 (Zero Gamma)
You said:
Zero Gamma = 6785
That level often acts like a regime switch.
Above 6785
Dealers typically:
Long gamma
Hedging dampens volatility
What that means intraday:
Price tends to pin or chop
Mean reversion dominates
Scalps work better than swing holds
Below 6785
Dealers flip:
Short gamma
Hedging amplifies moves
Intraday behavior:
Trend moves accelerate
Breaks follow through
Options expand rapidly
This is where the 300–1000% option expansions you keep referencing usually occur.
3. What This Means For Your SPX Option Plan
Scenario A — Market holds above 6785
Expect:
Range trading
Failed breakouts
Premium decay
Best play:
Quick scalps
Don’t expect big runners.

Rejection of resistance → quick put scalp
Support hold → quick call scalp

Scenario B — Market loses 6785

Directional acceleration
Option convexity
