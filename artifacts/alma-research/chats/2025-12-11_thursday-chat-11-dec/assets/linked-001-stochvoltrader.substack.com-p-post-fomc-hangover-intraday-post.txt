Statement says they will carefully assess “the extent and timing” of additional adjustments – language normally used when they’re signaling only a pause of QT rather than a new full easing cycle. Powell explicitly framed this as the third “risk-management” cut, aimed at stabilizing a labor market that has slowed sharply, while inflation is still above 2% and boosted by tariffs.   I don’t see a deep cutting cycle unless the labor data really cracks.    In the weekly post I wrote:    “Monday is expected to be a cautiously malign day, market prepositions itself for the week, while Tuesday is hedging and waiting for the FOMC.   On FOMC day I expect the Fed to be very “father figure like” hawkish to warn about the reality and risks. This is a “reconstructive” tone. Market will reprice the easing cycle, ultraoptimism if any.    [Powell was more dovish than expected, but warned about the reality!]    Thursday is the day of profit taking and rebalancing of previous trades (mostly causing sme couter-moves)…”     Here we are. Don’t be biased!   And don’t be afraid of a potential downside retest. Structurally, as long as SPX does not break 6789.19 on daily, the rebound continues. Interstingly enough, market done sold significant amount of puts around this line…    If this area would be broken and confirmed, the market would go to 6450/6400.
