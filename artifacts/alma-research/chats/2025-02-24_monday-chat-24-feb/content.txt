the key is, that: there is a collection of counterparties acting independently form each other, and the effect of their net activity aggregates. Different counterparties have different offsetting needs if there is any. But for us, traders, there is no edge on monitoring individual firms separately.
I know that MMs’ risk exposures are fluid, often adjusted on a second-by-second basis, and so any snapshot is only a temporary state. Their profit comes from managing the bid/ask spread.
...
The key is, again, that the firms inventory balance nets.
And all what we are looking at is the net effect. This is all what we are interested in, and what matters from tape-reading perspective.
We are not MMs, or brokers to monitor their activities in every detailes.
SEC, CFTC obligates MM firms to be risk neutral, and to report aggregate datas (that one can buy)..

In simplified terms, its the same as when you trade on spot directions, but in vol trading, instead of the spot direction, you trade vol direction.. implied vol.
For spot trading you mainly focus on the delta, gamma and charm of your contract,
in vol trading you watch vega, vomma and veta.
+vanna for both, bcs the game is still three dimensional.
...
think in numbers. basically... the contract value changes for certain numbers in a certain way. the greeks: delta, vega, theta describe these changing behaviors.
when you trade on spot, you speculate on the spot input changes on your contract. (bcs don't forget: YOU TRADE THE OPTION, NOT THE UNDERLYING!)
but when you trade on vol, you speculate on the vol input effects on your contract value.
this is the 'beauty' of options that you can short/long vol and short/long time.
...
shorting the smirk this time simply means that we bet on an overpricing... over-asked vol risk premium decay.
