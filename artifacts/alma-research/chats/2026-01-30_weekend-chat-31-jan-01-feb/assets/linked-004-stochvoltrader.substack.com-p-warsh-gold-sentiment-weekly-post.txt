Things are changing even faster.    Trump, Warsh & Duquesne    Last week, Trump nominated Kevin Warsh as the next Fed Chair. There has been extensive speculation about the significance of this move, because the prevailing narrative is that Warsh is a well‑known hawk. However, it is not that simple.                                           Under Warsh, a “front‑end easing + back‑end discipline” regime will emerge—one that would favor a curve steepener, rising term premia, and increased vulnerability in liquidity hedges (gold/crypto). Warsh wants to support the real economy, but not through QE or balance‑sheet expansion, because that distorts price discovery, creates a “Fed put” perception, carries inflationary side effects, and is politically exposed.   This regime also comes with an AI/productivity narrative and a Druckenmiller–Duquesne network backdrop. If you want to sell the idea that you can cut rates without triggering a renewed inflation scare, you need a “productivity step‑change (AI/tech)” story. Karp/Palantir is the poster child for that.   But if balance‑sheet discipline pushes term premia higher, lifts long‑end yields, and the market begins to slide, then—by the internal logic of the Fed, the presidency, and the mandate—easing eventually becomes unavoidable. And that will happen soon.   That’s the scheme here…                         Subscribe            (In many places right now, the   Karsan’s Lauder theory   that leads nowhere is what’s circulating. So, market sees shorter spot/vol beta, but also shorter IV/RV beta on IV down/RV up, until Friday. What market is betting on with its long skew trades is that call selling will go on and will be complacent, while it is ready to capitalize on downside moves, and it doesn’t expect at all on breaking through the downside target; it locally bets on short rel.vol heavily into Friday.    Above the centroid, fragility is expected: in case of a risk off impulse, everything moves into the same direction. While below the centroid, spot meets passive supportive flows.    So, overall, the market expects strong supportive forces below the centroid range towards the downside target. Above, the market sees very fragile and volatile environment, that it doesn’t expect to calm down. The OTM call selling is being seen as too aggressive and directly causing pressure on the spot (see vanna and zomma exposure), while it doesn’t see panic and bearish sentiment reversal, rather downside put decay again, with doesn’t really bet on too much upside.    The overall bet hence is a rangebound, with net negative drift but not breaking the structure.
