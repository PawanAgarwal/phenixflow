USD has lacked momentum for months. Tactical bias is mildly USD-bearish, but conviction is capped by rising cut odds and the data vacuum from the US shutdown.    There is a risk of BoJ hike in October. Thus I abandon the near-term long-JPY idea, not chasing JPY lower unless there’s fresh negative BoJ guidance. Takaichi won the LDP leadership (Oct 4–5) and is poised to become Japan’s first female PM. Her win can delay—but not derail—BoJ hikes, so it cannot be ruled out.                         Subscribe           Market positioning is overweight but not stretched overall, systematics elevated, and mega-cap tech heavy. The risk is in the equity markets (mostly tech). I see tech inflows resuming and financials/industrials also drawing money in certain weeks. US MMFs assets are sitting near record highs (~$7.37Trl in early Oct), indicating abundant cash that can be reallocated. Household checkable deposits/currency also remain elevated vs. pre-pandemic levels enabling the rotation. This, plus the decaying recession fears pushed inflows higher.   I treat the systematic-high/discretionary-meh mix as fragile, leaning into dispersion (single-name vol > index vol) and keeping defined-risk downside given the CTA asymmetric risk on drawdowns.
