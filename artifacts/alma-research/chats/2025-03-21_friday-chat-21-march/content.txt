I see some back and forth on things, confusing intraday local dealer hedging flow effects with non-local non-intraday dealer hedging flow effects.
There is difference between intraday local effects and wider-term net effects, that are derived solely from dealer hedging mechanism. But there are other flows in the market as well.

For example, just like now, some plp can say that "you said suppressive flows in yesterday post!! but now you say 'oh i said supportive opex' so you are right anyway".
Nope...
I'm talking about supportive march opex since feb, even despite the respected furus are talked about doomsday, flush etc. for this week.
The LOCAL INTRADAY vanna charm flows were suppressive yesterday at a certain range in terms of not letting any rally, not letting the upward move to gain momentum.
(You have to understand what these flows mean in order to understand this. I link the OD chat in every intraday post. Take your time.)
But the OVERALL positioning is supportive into this OpEx + as I keep saying: HF algos are in mean reversion mode as you can see in the price action clearly.

You have to learn to think in different tenors paralel. So for example while locally your portfolio gains delta that you neutralize, on a hourly, or daily tenor it still can lose delta that you have to buy back later. (Very much simplified)
NOTHING is moving in straight linear lines!
This is where most of the retails are lost.

This community is full of high knowledge plp. Please, ask proper questions after you made it sure that you didn't find answers in my posts or in the internet... in order to avoid wasting other's time as a result of simple laziness for thinking.

Great question. The approach is fairly similar, but the option pricing always also includes market factors that influence the distribution. When there is a lot of supply on a certain point or area of the vol surface that distorts the distribution. Also it would make it by definition impossible to benefit from long or short vol because your market model and your option price model are based on the exact same distribution. What you want to profit from (the edge Alma is referring to) is the mispricing of the real odds, e.g. the market prices a different outcome than the underlying statistical process would have you expect. So in practice what you do is you configure the statistical process (the Wiener process/Brownian motion) to have fatter tails, for example by including random jumps).
