WEEKLY POSTHere is the weekly positioning:

This is a net supportive downside bet with upside vol selling. The most supportive area is clustering around 6670, where volatility is expected to brake any downside move.Upside vol selling is not that steep, ranther around 6860, but the positioning rather says that “we want to diminish left-tail as much as we can, and create a slightly resistance-less path to the upside towards 7000, but the now the focus must be on protecting the downside”. The reason is that Fed cut is expected to be hawkish and critical against the policy, as Fed is blindfolded now.

Centroid area is at 6836.23 - 6841.98Upside pivot is at 6927.53 with target of 6986.74Downside pivot is at 6763.18 with target of 6669.73

Positioning breakdownNet gamma is negative. Customers bet on convexity.Net color is also negative, so they expect the convexity to increase into Friday. Net speed is also negative, customers sell downside vol and buy upside vol (but the emphasis is on downside vol selling).

Net vega is negative, butNet veta is positive, so market expects a cap IV into Friday, andNet vanna is also positive. And this is very telling! Because this along with the others tells me, that market expects rather ‘volatility scalpings’ on the put side into FOMC, rather than real left-tail moves. Now also very important to say, that vanna switches to negative above ~5% vol increase. This reveals us the net target where market jumps into scalping the left-tail bets. Net vomma is also positive. And this completely congruent with what the previous functions just said. Volatility is expected to be capped, and rangebounding.

Vomma supply levels:6742.17 - 6783.33, this is the area what the expected downside selling projects;6957.44 - 6998.60, projected upside vol selling area. These areas pretty much coincidencing with the speed profile outputs.This doesn’t necessary mean that the spot should stop here, but this is where the market expects the momentum to be braked and “vol down” move to kick in.

Fixed strike skew change is net negative, especially to the upside. Tail skew is increased by 19.85%, and 22-delta skew by 21.44%, but this change is the result of the net put and call selling, not the bid of the tails. While on a fix strike vol basis, the whole complex is down by 5.54% in avg. This is exactly what we were seeing in the vega positioning and speed profile.

So, all in all, the sentiment is pretty consistently waiting for a quick liquidity grab into FOMC towards 6670 at max, in the worst case, not a trend shift for now. This is the main bet.However this is mostly a volatility trade rather than a spot trade, because the sentiment is expected to be tense ahead of FOMC, due to the concern about sustainability of the easing cycle. Even tho the vol models project levels, the market’s expected main edge is to sell the downside IV. They don’t bet on a big downside spot move, rather they bet on net upside in terms of delta trades. This is reinforced by the asymmetry in the positioning: the downside IV grab expectation is heavy, while upside vol bets are more modest.For real left-tail something very bad news should occur or no cut with hawkish guidance, that would break these targets opening the door towards 6400 and even to 6200 after. Even tho the probability is very, but never zero.
