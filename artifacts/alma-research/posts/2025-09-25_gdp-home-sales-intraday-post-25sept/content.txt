Window of Risk, Disbalance - Investigating the story that the market tells

Futures are trading at 6618 when I’m writing this post…I called for Window of Risk opening at post-OpEx Tuesday since August, now the market has shaken, but nothing is proven yet. As said, the key trigger is the inflation. More details is in the post above.

We have GDP, Home sales and druable goods orders MoM release today. The consensus about GDP is little or no revision to the second‑estimate reading of 3.3% annualised growth. Home Sales is expected to report sales around 3.93/98 million annualised units, down from 4.01 million in July. Affordability remains the main brake—30‑year mortgage rates near 7% keep buyers on the sidelines despite gradual easing.

Meanwhile SPX/yields correlation is neutral:

But the trend is up, so short-term sentiment is shifting towards “bad news are bad news, good news are good news”.The annual comprehensive revisions to GDP will also be released; they could change the historic profile even if the Q2 headline stays at 3.3%

PCE tomorrow is way more important. It is expected to come at 0.28–0.35% m/m. This is because of shelter, while PCE has heavier weights on goods prices, which fell.Anyway, a print above 0.25% m/m would push three‑month annualised core PCE above 3.5%, raising doubts about disinflation.

Upside pivot is 6652.38The spot can move upward more freely, upside target is 6665.72 - 6673 range.This should provide a nice reversion trade opportunity, back towards the centroid. But, the market’s main bet is a ranging around the center of the fly, testing the downside support and a reversion from the upside target.
