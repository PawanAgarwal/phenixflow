Summary first

Over the past several months — essentially since last autumn — I have repeatedly emphasised that the risk of an Iranian military offensive is elevated in the window between the February and March OPEX periods.This is completely independent of the broader risk accumulation we expect toward the end of spring. It is an external, geopolitical, war-related risk.This April-May call did not change, only the magnitude of the risk has changed.I expect the Iranian aggression to peak around the OPEX week. However, based on the current global geopolitical environment, the most dangerous period for the West will be the window from April to May, as the economy will have become significantly more vulnerable by then.Geopolitical tensions will seemingly ease slightly during the summer, with the next critical window opening in November — by which time even more geopolitical risks will have accumulated than we are currently facing this spring.

Strait of Hormuz

Currently, the most critical phase of the conflict is unfolding in the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman, primarily in the electromagnetic spectrum.the IRGC is continuously broadcasting on open channels, declaring that “the Strait of Hormuz is closed, no vessel may pass.” Although this has not been officially declared, a de facto blockade is already in effect.Large-scale military GPS jamming and widespread AIS spoofing are ongoing. Phantom vessels are appearing on ship radars, dramatically increasing the risk of collisions and the chance that commercial ships could be misidentified by naval forces and engaged.According to my sources, more than 450 oil and gas tankers (LNG/LPG), 200 bulk carriers, and 170 container ships are currently stranded in the Gulf. Bloomberg data supports these figures.

Importantly, the vessels are not stopping solely because they cannot physically transit the strait. Many are being held back due to charterparty terms, banking requirements, P&I and war risk insurance conditions, as well as port and flag-state restrictions. This has created a de facto standstill in maritime traffic. Transit has become economically and legally impossible without proper insurance coverage.However, due to radio jamming and spoofed signals, accurate real-time data is not reaching Bloomberg or other market intelligence platforms. As a result, the geo-risk premium on oil and gas products has spiked by as much as 50%.

According to leaked diplomatic reports, regional leaders have warned Washington that oil prices above $100 now represent a “clear and present danger” — despite OPEC+’s decision to increase production, because the physical supply remains locked inside the Gulf.Additionally, Gulf states have halted put trading on exchanges until Tuesday across the GCC.

The second key flashpoint is the Saudi oil refineries in the KSA, which Iranian forces are repeatedly attempting to reach. Both are high-beta risks, but the second one in particular carries extreme danger, as it could take the Petroline pipeline out of commission entirely.If refinery gets hit, it can slowdown the oil price increase, but generate a spike in gas prices.
