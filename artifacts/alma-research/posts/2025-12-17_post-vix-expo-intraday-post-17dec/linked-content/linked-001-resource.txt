WEEKLY POSITIONING

Customers are selling left-tail pretty much for this opex, while upside vol is barely bid. The positioning is more about “protect the downside area, juicing the liquidity up there, rather than betting on upside squeezes”.

The centroid of the speed profile is right at the gap: 6753.47 - 6778.24Not a coincidence! Big pivot to see if dip buyers step in or they let the market stabilize lower.

The positioning puts the downside pivot at 6686.66, with target of 6604.58Unnecessary to say, that in this area, market can be expected to be sticky and rangebounding.

The upside pivot is at 6896.27, with target of 6962.42The upside vacuum is very flat, that can provide a relatively stable upward move if it happens. Let’s look at the greeks…

Greeks of the positioningNet gamma is slightly positive, market bets on significant swings mostly below the upside pivot.Net color is flat-to-positive but switches to negative into Friday. Telling us that market expect some very unstable liquidity to be built over the week, but more room for swings after CPI release. Net zomma is positive, generating a negative correlation between IV and RV, this indicates sentiment-traps ahead, and vol scalping.

Net vega is negative over the week. Net veta is negative until Wednesday, and flips to positive into Thursday and Friday. This means IV is expected to be bid until data releases, then MMs will need to sell it. Net vomma, on Monday and Tuesday is negative until ±5% change in vol and flips to positive above. This provides resistance for vol moves. After Wednesday it flips to net negative, amplifying any vol moves. Net vanna is flat-to-negative, decaying to zero into Friday, meaning market expects more vol upside and more liquidity downside.

This is like having a fly spread on volatility into Wednesday with MM negative ultima down, positive ultima up, and after a risk reversal with MM net flat-to-negative ultima.(Ultima is like “speed for vol”, if vomma is the “gamma of vol”.) This tells me, that vol traders expect volatility to rangebound into VIX expo but being overvalued, providing a good opportunity them to scalp it. And after data release, they are more like betting on a vol up move, that is expected to be undervalued. Slightly more odds for that, but given the relatively flat ultima, not too much more odds. If we can believe to the positioning and assume that it is not complacent, then it means that the spot will squeeze up towards the upside pivot. But for me, this also tells that the market expect that the spot will collect some “fuel” before this move to happen, with spot grinding downwards, or at least being below 6820 until data release. Simply because traders are net short the skew, and expect to scalp it.

This also means that VIX expo is suppressive for equitites if VIX is above 16.32 into expo. This is a key pivot for VIX. If breaks above, we will see vol buyback flow into Wednesday, if stays below and grinds lower, we will see vol selling flow after the expo.

So all-in-all, the market bets on that spot will find liquidity around the gap, and expects that the customers will overreact it and so they can scalp the skew. After that, they expect an inline or slightly cooler data print, and they can lock upside vol premium in.
