PPI, OpEx, Intraday post (15/May)

PPI + SPX/LTY correlationEconomists forecast the April PPI to rise 0.2% month-over-month, a modest uptick after recent tariff-related shocks and y/y PPI to hold near 2.5%, given the lagged effects of commodity swings and tariff pass-through. Fed‐funds futures now price near‐zero odds of a June cut and push the first easing into July, even as ECB insiders now see a June rate reduction as probable but expect only a 25 bp move, not a large adjustment, and the BoE just cut to 4.25%, setting the stage for additional quarter‐point moves later in the year. PPI outcomes could nudge but not derail the Fed’s hold decision.

Now, you have to understand, that the market wants to get rid of recessionary fears. They concentrate on growth now that is equal to inflation as well now (stagflation). This is why, over the past few weeks, whenever the quant models suggested a healthy pullback, the government literally intervened and released benign announcements to trigger supportive buying. Why? Simply because the deflationary risk is real. They need to keep it going until the planned events unfold and investment interest strengthens. If you followed my April post, you know.A stronger PPI print suggests robust demand and firmer pricing power, alleviating some recession fears even as it keeps inflation risks alive. This is why we see increased SPX/LTY correlation. Soft print now means growth concerns, while strong print means growth optimism. But upside is capped due to stagflation. Rollover risk, supply‐shock inflation, and layoff fears are looming there that will roil fixed-income more than equities.
