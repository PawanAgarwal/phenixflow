Market has finally seriously shaken, but structure has not broken. It was tested but market held the marked lower bound at 6650.13
The reason I say ‘seriously shaken’ is that the sell wasn’t the cause of mechanical flows but strictly sentiment-driven. As I keep warning you over the past 2 weeks, market has a lot of mispriced risk, plus the admin’s position is also challenged: 1) as I showed you, the AI/tech sector has significant misvaluations and faces supply-chain risk; 2) Trump’s position is weakening he is losing leverage against the ‘deep state’ and against his foreign opponents.
Based on some intels, I estimate serious troubles for him into next Q1, late February-March period, and market will feel it, because all what happened since April was built on the faked reality that the admin projected, and that the public trusts less and less now… this is the key! This is a big sentiment pivot.
I don’t expect bear market, but that period is my next window of risk target, after this current one.

The risk/liquidity proxy asset, Bitcoin also broke my marked 107k pivot, and most likely heading towards my marked downside targets. In my July post, I predicted topping into August and volatility after October. Voilá, it is happening… targets are still the same. Structurally, it should rebound now back towards 112-115k and lose structure there to confirm downward move for the coming months.
This coming week is risk heavy: first, we have OpEx, but before that we have VIX expiration, NVDA earnings and NFP release.
Me personally, I’m not into shorting this now, at least not until NVDA ER, the guide matters as it can amplify the depreciation/ROI concerns.
My personal short-term strat is rather building long positions into Dec rally (potentially triggered by further Fed cut), with primarly not expecting new ATHs.
My bias for a Santa rally is intact yet. The more lower the market is let to go, the better and more stable the rally will be. But from now, every rally will be considered very fragile.
Market is starting to wake up from an euphoria, and will realize that it has outrun itself. It is a house of cards. The trust is shaken, looking for the reality. Trump wants to avoid blackswan (I think he will), but his leverage is running out of ammo.
Plus datas are back, and as I said, data is very risky now…

Let’s see, what can we expect from OpEx, and what levels, pivots and structure the positioning projects ahead…

WEEKLY POST
Here I coded the weekly positioning:

Centroid, pivots
We have a dealer fly pattern, with short ratio call spread upwards and long put spread downwards, centering around 6717.14 (almost the same as last week) drawing a net negative speed profile. This makes 6715/20 range locally pretty supportive.
The centroid is in the 6751.74 - 6799.83 range, this is the point where we can expect significant dynamic-shift, and magnetic center.
Upside pivot is at 6834.14, with acceleration towards 6894.92 max weekly target.
Downside pivot is at 6623.17, with downside target of 6436.83
Both pivot is out of the range that the vol market sentiment counts on for the next week. So, any direction plays out, the structure will be broken.
Let’s see the dynamics, priced-in…

Dealer exposure
The profile is very sensitive.
Net short gamma gives room for realized volatility, mostly to the upside, but below the center of the fly, to the downside as well, but way less.
Net long color means gamma will increase as time passes, I code it that into Wednesday the net gamma flips into positive, making the center even more supportive, and brakes any upside squeezes.

Net short vega says that the profile is volatility sensitive, but
Net veta exposure is positive into Wednesday, making dealers profile longer vega, having a vol suppressive effect. But veta flips to negative after as vega flips to positive, and this means that into the second half, we can expect vol expansion.
Net vomma is also negative, and very much negtive, the exposure is big. This means that IV movements are amplified, and are very much sensitive.
Net vanna is negative here, providing less resistance and less mechanical liquidity upward and supportive flows downward.
Net zomma exposure is negative, so IV/RV correlation is more positive, but very low impact is expected.

The first two days are pivotal, even Tuesday close into Wednesday opening. The centroid should be taken out otherwise it will be even harder to climb up, and spot will be sucked towards the center of the fly.
Veta and vanna will support upward squeeze towards the pivot, but only if the centroid is taken back. Otherwise market will range around the center, with some overshootings to the downside within the structure, supported by short vomma.
Market bets on right skewed and heavily leptokurtic distribution for the week.
Plus even if a squeeze plays out after NVDA ER, the rally would not be sustainable. I suspect, that would be taken back into some local magnets, but I will revise the OpEx position in the Thursday premarket post.

Fix strike skew
On a fix strike basis, the volatility is up by avg 9.47%, mostly at the 22-delta strikes. However the customers are slightly short skew at the tails, first because of profit taking, and also because they estimate low probability that the downside pivot will be broken as they expect decreasing volatility there and more buying interest there.
The 22-delta skew is however bid but not extremely, so hedging is there against local volatility towards the downside pivot at max.
