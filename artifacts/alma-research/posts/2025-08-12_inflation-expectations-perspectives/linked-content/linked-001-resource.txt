WEEKLY POSTLet’s look at the August OpEx positioning first:

The net speed exposure hides a short fly position with heavily skewed to the right tail. This is pretty supportive now, because negative speed profiles prevent markets to fall. What market is betting on is basically liquidity between the centroid area and the downside pivot. It bets that market won’t fall through 6230.The centroid area is 6395.48 - 6384.88 where the magnetic force will be the most pronunced, and 6435.37 is from where the market expects right tail towards 6483.50 (zo confirm further upward momentum, this line should be broken; this is already a beningn CPI bet). But 6435.37 is more likely a reversion back towards the centroid of the fly structure… except if inflation numbers are very disinflationary and geo hopes are well-grounded (fix strike skew is longer but fix strike vol is sold pretty much into CPI day)…And downside pivot is at 6269.55, this line serves as a borderline where spot can escape the centroid magnet, and go lower towards 6230. But market doesn’t place too much bet on this.

A very important thing is that zomma is positive. So whenever the vol wants to catch up into any direction, the momentum literally caps itself, bcs it flattens the speed profile to the upside, and steepens it to the downside. So this means, market cannot go anywhere with increased vol too far. Volatility needs to be sold, and once it is sold, that will be reinforced by local negative vomma, and market can be stabilized. Imagine this like, here increased vol cuts off the right-tail, makes this tail smaller, while increases the body of the distribution on the left. Volatility is managed. Downside is managed.And after the OpEx we will see mechanical buyback flow, especially if the spot is above the centroid, because thats where long charm is the most pronunced. This was already expected in the monthly post too, and fix strike skew also shows skew selling right after the OpEx.
