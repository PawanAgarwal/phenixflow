First lets look at the SPX/LTY beta:

Market shows less and less fear of inflation now, but beta is still negative, meaning, a soft CPI print (or any print within the expectations) typically drives yields lower (as it stokes Fed‐easing bets) and lifts equities.Analysts broadly expect May CPI to show mild headline inflation (+0.16% m/m; +2.4% y/y) and core inflation (+0.27% m/m; +2.9% y/y). Persistent readings above 0.3% m/m could force the Fed to delay cuts further, while soft print buys time for Fed cuts and a quieter Q3. Any upside surprise would trigger stairstep selloff into midsummer tariff shock. If CPI stays sticky while growth slows, the Fed faces persistent stagflation and bearish Q3.
