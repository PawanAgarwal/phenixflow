After Thursday’s price action, the SPX would need a roughly 4% rally to trigger both medium-term and short-term systematic buying levels. Once that occurs, nearly $70 billion of U.S. equities could be unlocked for purchase. Despite recent declines, market breadth is strong—which isn’t surprising if you understand the context I keep writing about.

On the institutional side, however, hedge fund gross leverage is at an all‑time high. When prices dip, forced de‑grossing (i.e., selling) may happen, making trading choppy. The ratio of short sales to long sales is about 5.2 to 1, meaning there are over five short sales for every long sale. This large imbalance reflects a heavy bear skew in market sentiment. Of the 11 U.S. sectors, 7 were net sold. The recent drop in U.S. Fundamental L/S Net leverage to roughly two-year lows shows that the overall aggressiveness of long and short positions has fallen sharply.More importantly, the aggregate long/short ratio (longs divided by shorts) has hit its lowest level in over five years. The top sectors being sold were Information Technology, Consumer Discretionary, Industrials, and Financials—generally more cyclical or growth-oriented.All in all, the market remains full of uncertainty and high institutional leverage.

In April, there are new tariffs scheduled for April 2nd and 15th, and Trump has already started discussing nuclear issues with Iran, which is a highly sensitive topic.There’s talk on Wall Street that real QE might arrive in May. I think this is still early, but if everything goes according to plan, it’s possible—though the market must drop significantly first. Meanwhile, funds plan to offset their significant losses through tax loss harvesting around April OpEx week—no coincidence there.A personal meeting between Putin and Trump is scheduled for after Ramadan and Eid.Volatility on a fixed-strike basis has significantly decreased going into April, and the skew is shorter. Complacency is always what leads to sharp declines, not when the market is hedged.

I’m not sure what will happen between March OpEx and end of month. For now, I’m taking it day by day. But in any case, the bottom isn’t in yet—only a temporary one.

The net slopeness of the speed profile is -0.32% that counts as netural-to-suppressive.

Why is it suppressive?This means that dealers are net longer puts and shorter calls, creating a net short delta exposure, which they hedge by buying futures. However, vanna and charm exposures are shifted: the former is net negative, and the latter is net positive.This implies that while short delta does induce a buying flow, if volatility falls, dealers become longer delta. On one hand, that reduces the buying flow; on the other hand—if vol becomes high enough—it can lead to cutting back long ES exposure. Moreover, as time passes, they inevitably become longer delta, which also cancels out the supportive buyback flow effect.So we shouldn’t really expect a rally—at most a squeeze, where increasing volatility could help the bull move, temporary.

(By “squeeze,” I mean spot up/vol up.)For the bears, 5532 is the level where they should begin to feel the longer downside gamma effect, and from there, 5500 acts as a strong magnet.

So, there are two different processes at work. One operates slowly in the background, while the other locally determines portfolio values. For example:The neutral-to-suppressive overall speed profile gradually reduces the total net portfolio value over time when volatility falls. Yet, because the speed profile slope is only -0.32%, the effect is quite small. It doesn't support any major, strong, sustained bull rally, and it doesn't directly push prices down either—it's weak but sets the tone, preventing any strong bull run.Local effects within the overall net MM portfolio define the immediate momentum moves. For instance, above the vanna flip line, net vanna is longer; between 5625 and 5700, charm is short; and past the net charm flip level, net charm becomes negative.In other words, if volatility can decline (and the locally positive color suggests it might), that could be supportive up to 5700. However, if volatility does fall, the overall portfolio delta increases slightly, which compresses every upside move. Is it clear?
