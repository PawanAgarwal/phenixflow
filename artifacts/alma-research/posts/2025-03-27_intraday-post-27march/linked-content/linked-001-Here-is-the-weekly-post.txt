Now that we're past OpEx, we need to determine what the market wants until the end-of-month expiry. And this is where the JPM collar will come into effect. So now I write a bit about it again in advance.

JPM Put Spread CollarJPM hedge equity operation actually consists of three funds, with JHEQX being the largest. These funds operate continuously and have overlapping expirations.Unlike a traditional protective collar trade, this fund uses a debit spread. The extra premium gained from selling the put option lets the manager raise the call strike and increase the cap. This spread protects against losses beyond roughly 5%, but after about 20% losses, the protection ends and performance tracks the market one‑to‑one. It does not offer coverage against extreme tail events. Also they use SPXW options (EOM, PM‑settled), so execution and resetting are tied to the closing settlement.

Once the collar is established around 2 PM (the official settlement), the fund managers do not actively adjust or roll the position. This is a common misconception. Instead, the collar simply expires, and they reset it with a new one. There’s no active monetization of the collar during its lifetime. Since the new collar adds further short delta exposure, they also open a 0DTE call leg to neutralize that delta.See the official brochure here.

Here you can see the 4700/5565/6165 collar on EOM expo:

Above the long put strike, the position is a short delta, long vega, short theta, long charm, short vanna, short vomma trade, while below the long put strike, it’s a short delta, short vega, long theta, short charm, short vanna, long vomma trade.What this means is that, if spot is above the long put strike, MMs are long vanna, short charm, long vomma, and if spot is below the long put strike, MMs are short vanna, long charm, short vomma.Now, if spot is above the put strike (5565); if vol decreases MMs become shorter vega and shorter delta (due to long vanna), and the positive vanna convexity (zomma) is increasing due to their long vomma exposure, until ~5858-5870 range. And if the spot is trading below the put strike (5565); by vol decrease, MMs become longer vega and longer delta (due to short vanna), and the positive vanna convexity (zomma) is increasing until the ~5110-5115 range due to theri short vomma exposure. What the steepening positive zomma indicates is that, as volatility declines and spot is moving upwards (above the long put leg) or downwards (below the long put leg), changes in delta accelerate, which increases the market makers’ hedging needs—namely, buying back more futures when the market is long vanna, or selling off more futures when the market is short vanna.The exact opposite happens if volatility increases.

This creates a mechanical gravitational effect toward one of the short legs of the collar trade (the MM long leg), either 4700 or 6165. This doesn’t mean the spot will actually be pulled toward those exact strikes; it only means that a vol crush generates a very strong mechanical supportive or suppressive flow (depending on whether the spot is trading above or below the long put strike). That flow is further backed by charm flows, which become more and more pronounced as we approach expiration.
