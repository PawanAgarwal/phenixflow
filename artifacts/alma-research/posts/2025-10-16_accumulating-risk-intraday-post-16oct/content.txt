Weekly post, october OpEx:

OpEx week, China risk - Weekly post (13 - 17/Oct)

We have a lot of geo risks not being properly priced in. The rate cuts serve to mask these fragilities. If markets sniff balance-sheet flexibility, they won’t assume growth “stimulus”; they’ll assume floor under liquidity—i.e., lower left-tail vol, not higher earnings. That helps explain why risk rallies fade when geopolitics worsen: the “shock absorber” keeps plumbing working, but doesn’t fix risk premia. There are new covert ops in Venezuela with escalation paths (sanctions → covert action → kinetic) that usually richen front-end equity vol, bid gold, and flatten curves in the first instance. And the market is vulnerable to a disorderly correction if policy/geopolitical shocks collide with stretched valuations and deficits. HY OAS is only ~3.1%; MOVE sits mid-70s — elevated enough to transmit macro shocks to equities, but not “panic,” which can breed complacency. Not priced in at all…Institutions are slowly accumulating puts against retails, despite crowded short vol trade into the OpEx (VIXpiry next week), CTAs are expected to reduce exposure. Tactically owning downside protection into near-term event risks is clever, with a plan to fade vol as blackout ends and corporate demand returns.Never forget, what I wrote in the weekly post and the ‘window of risk’ post: market is again headline sensitive!
