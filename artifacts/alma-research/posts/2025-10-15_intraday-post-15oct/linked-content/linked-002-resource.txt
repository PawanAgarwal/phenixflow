Let’s look at the OpEx positioning…

WEEKLY POSITIONINGHere you can see the weekly positioning:

This is a net positive speed profile, also we have net long vanna and net short charm into the Friday expo. I don’t see convexity shifts, so no fly pattern on the horizont. This speed profile means, that to the upside the gamma is increasing, and it is decreasing to the downside. The steepness is not too significant. Making it easier for the spot to climb upward, but volatility should be sold, telling us that the spot ‘accepts the environment’. This comes hand in hand with longer vanna profile, meaning that mechanically the dealer profile becomes longer vega if price climbs upward, making them to sell volatility. And if volatility is sold, they become shorter delta, making them to buy futures supporting the spot to the upside. Meanwhile, negative charm flow makes dealers shorter delta as time passes, generating a relatively weak passive buyback flow into the OpEx. This flow will be significant on Thursday and Friday.

The net gamma and net vega exposures of the profile are both negative.So this creates a very vol-sensitive (“left-tail sensitive”) positioning. This is mitigated a bit by the net long color (gamma decay) exposure, but I estimate only a 16-17% gamma increase over the week, still remaining in the negative territory. The profile is also long veta and short vomma. And this is a very important part!The long veta (vega decay) profile creates a passive and decreasing vol selling flow into Wednesday. VIX pin at 21s is very likely into VIX expo, but I will reassume it on Tuesday. However the short vomma (gamma of vega) exposure makes the profile even more sensitive to vol squeezes. (Below, I share the areas that the profile implies for vol crush and vol squeeze)

However, the most important part of the profile for me is, that we are in long zomma territory! And it is even longer to the downside. Zomma is the gamma change to volatility. It is only positive at the very tails. It means vol up/gamma up, vol down/gamma down. This is a mathematically inbuilt mechanism, because the system is designed to not let volatility to be squeezed to infinity, or to be crushed to zero. Because if vol spikes, downside gammas will go up as well, flattening the speed profile and generating a “brake” effect to the downside through long gamma hedging (trading futures against the direction compressing the intraday realized returns). When I see long zomma, it always tells me that market expects that the spot reached the very tails of the local curve on the scrutinized tenor. In this case, this is the left-tail:

(just imagine the functions pushed lower on the y-axis until the gamma and vomma curves reaches negative territory)This is why I don’t see the fly pattern, because the center would be even more higher, where vanna and speed shift to negative.So this tells me, that the market doesn’t expect more downside, unless more macro trigger (China) occurs. If it does, sub-6400 is the target.
