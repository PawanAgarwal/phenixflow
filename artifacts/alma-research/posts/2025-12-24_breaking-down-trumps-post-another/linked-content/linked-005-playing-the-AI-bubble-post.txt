In modern markets, one can use repurchase agreements (repos) to unlock liquidity from bonds. In a repo transaction, bondholders can effectively convert their bonds to cash temporarily: they sell the bond to a lender and agree to repurchase it later, using the bond as collateral. This practice enables large institutions to unlock money parked in treasuries or other high-quality bonds, freeing cash for other investments without outright selling their bond holdings.
