Bitcoin analysis for the next 5 years

Investment, Cryptomarket cycle breakdown

As you know, I don’t do short-term speculative trades with Bitcoin. The main reason is that its fundamentals are not well defined yet, and its trend is much slower than that of the stock market. This may sound strange given the difference in their volatilities, but if you watch BTC, what it mostly does is one big, sharp move toward a target, followed by a multi-week range-bound consolidation there.

Right now I’m scaling down part of my BTC exposure in my portfolio, because in the future, once it reaches my target level, I want to use those profits to increase Bitcoin’s weight to 10%, maybe even higher. The fundamentals of Bitcoin, together with those of the equity market, are going to change, and that’s why I plan to treat Bitcoin as an investment going forward, rather than as a well-paying, long-term, high-risk speculative trade. The reason is that the stock market is intended to be integrated into the blockchain. When it happens, its risk will still be overestimated. I wrote about this in an earlier post – please go back and read it.

Playing the AI-Bubble; Long-term outlook

Let’s break it down…

The structureLocally, our most important support zone lies in the 74,113.30–81,297.74 range. The question is whether we get a 2021-style pattern with one more hit to the highs before we roll over into a bear market or not. The odds slightly favor a larger bounce, but first we need to see a stable structure being built in the support zone, and then get a bounce out of it.

Speaking in Elliott Wave terms, this is like the B “bull trap” wave of an ABC correction. The question is whether that B-wave extends or not. As I said, the odds lean slightly toward an extension, but we need confirmation around 106,348.41. That is the key pivot level. If BTC shows trend continuation there, we’re likely to see 138,241.88 and even 151,346.44 before the crypto market drops into a bear market.

The bear market starts once BTC breaks below the 74,113.30–81,297.74 support zone, ± volatility, which more or less lines up with the November 2021 top. If you think about it in terms of liquidity, that also looks quite logical.

As I said, I’m only interested in buying again below 60k. Quantitatively, my downside target zone is 40,621.76–48,388.37. Personally, I don’t care about any “extended B-wave”, as mentioned, I don’t take short-term speculative trades in BTC. My alarm only goes off once BTC has broken below 60k.The question is roughly when we can expect that target to be reached. Well, after running multiple time-series analyses on the very limited historical Bitcoin data we have, I keep arriving at two time windows:

One of them has already passed, and BTC formed a local top there. The second falls between late 2027 and mid-2030, which roughly lines up with the timeframe when I expect the stock market and the economy to roll over as well, under the burden of debt and the shadow of the inflated AI bubble – roughly a year before the next elections. I say “roughly lines up” because my estimate for the economy and the equity market is much tighter and is skewed more toward 2030. Besides time-series analysis, other informations also point in that direction.

SummaryAll in all, my bias is that during 2026 we’ll see an extended, pretty boring B-wave, followed by a long C-wave that runs into roughly the middle of the late-2027 to mid-2030 window I marked. Then I’d expect a dull consolidation before it starts to grind higher again. The B-wave will be tested at the marked pivot line. It can lead to a multi-month consolidation with no new highs, or to new highs…From that point on, I expect BTC’s volatility to come down and its price action to look more “stock-market-ish”. It kind of has to, given that the slope of the arithmetic trendlines are already pushing above 89°…And for the next cycle (post-2030) my primal upside target will be 220,000 - 260,500 area.
