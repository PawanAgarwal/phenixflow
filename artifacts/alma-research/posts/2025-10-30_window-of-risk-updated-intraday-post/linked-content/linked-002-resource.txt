WEEKLY POSTLet’s scrutinize the weekly positioning:

The net speed profile is negative, with downside heavily sold towards 6559.55 as the most supportive cluster (weighted avg). The profile is net long gamma, long vega,long charm, short color, short vanna, short veta, long vommaand long zomma.

Breaking down the net dealer exposureLet’s go through them again,Net long gamma indicates that the dealer flow is net short on realized volatility, as they are net hedging against the spot movements, compressing the realized daily returns; Net short speed means that dealers are net long upside realized volatility (shorter upside gamma), and net short downside realized volatility (longer downside gamma). The speed profile is very convex to the downside, so this makes the left-tail of the implied distribution heavily sold;Net long charm means that dealers become longer delta as time passes, generating a slow passive selling flow (low impact, stronger into Friday); Net short color means that gamma decays as time passes. As I coded, above roughly ~6750 the net gamma goes negative into Wednesday, while it remains positive below. This steepens the upside speed convexity, and increases the spot-to-realized volatility correlation;Net short vanna indicates that dealers are shorter upside vega and longer downside vega, or in otherwords they are shorter delta if vol goes up, and longer delta if vol goes down. This pushes the spot/vol beta upward, but only slightly now, as vanna exposure is small;Net short veta tells us that the dealers become shorter vega as time passes making them buying back volatility into Wednesday/Thursday window. The vega exposure is very high from the downside, so this is impactful. However, the veta declines fast, the vega decay is slowing down as vega converges to zero, and so this makes the Monday-Tuesday window sensitive;Net long vomma keeps the implied volatility under control, because whenever the volatility spikes, dealers’ vega exposure rises forcing them to sell volatility and vice versa. It is like making the implied volatility rangebound, and so compressing the tailedness (kurtosis) of the implied distribution;Net long zomma means that the gamma and implied volatility correlation is net positive lowering the IV-to-RV correlation on a net basis. If implied vol goes up the gamma exposure increases, providing more pressure on realized volatility, and vice versa. This creates a tricky and trappy environment for traders who are net short both IV and RV (but relatively low significance due to the low vanna exposure);

Speed profile pivotsThe centroid of the speed profile is 6665.25;this is the ‘center of mass’ (so to say), the point where shorter upside gamma ‘vacuum’ and longer downside gamma ‘gravitational’ effects are in balance with each other. And this way, gives us the most important pivot for the market where only the sentiment affects the price. The distribution offers both possibiliy: well-supplied supportive downside and gamma squeezish upside, the sentiment will decide which one it chooses.

The downside pivot is at 6596.58 (margin of error is 6582.54) this is where the spot arrives into the strong gravitational field of the highly convex downside long gammas, with target of 6559.55

The upside pivot is at 6819.26, this is the line where the vacuum-up effect turns on and provide us a squeeze up towards 6918.28 gamma-wise.

Vomma and zomma based upside and downside target rangesNote, that short vanna makes the spot/vol beta longer (not necessary positive, I don’t see that much vanna), but zomma exposure is positive. So if implied volatility suppressed as spot grinds down, the realized volatility can increase as downside gamma lowers a bit. Same to the upside, as if implied vol catches a bid along with spot, the zomma will brake realized volatility by slightly increasing the upside gammas. Taking all of these into account along with implied vol of vol and gamma convexities, I take the implied volatility moves for the week, and code what price ranges it gives me from where the realized volatility is expected to be suppressed into both ways: Downside = 6648.57 - 6694.51Upside = 6856.69 - 6887.42

Why don’t these zones align with the pivots? Because these are primarly based on vomma. Implied vol of vol and expected/forecasted vol of vol implies a certain move in realized volatility. Taking zomma into account, these are the target ranges that the vol of vol bets imply. Kurtosis is sold, implied distribution is right-tailish.
