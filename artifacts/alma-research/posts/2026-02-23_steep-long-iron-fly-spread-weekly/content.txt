Let’s srutinize the expectations more in details.

Dealer exposureNet gamma is positive only on Monday then is shifts to heavily negative at the ATM, with tails remaining positive over the week. Net color is heavily negative, both around ATM and into the tails too. This creates a volatile vacuum towards the centroid. Downside gamma decays much slower durning the week, and this creates a stronger magnetic effect below the pivot. Net zomma is mostly positve, but it’s complex. Around the centroid, it is negative into Monday, Wednesday and Thursday, while positive on Tuesday and Friday. However, around the centroid is it flat. Downside zomma at the tails is positive and upside zomma as well (expect Friday, where it shifts to negative, with high impact). Local asymmetries are insignificant. This is like customers are buying an iron butterfly with vacuum in the middle expecting wide snapbacks and retest of key pivots. But meanwhile the positioning is ready to brake the momentum into both direction, however here, the downside is more bid. So, on a “vol up” mometum, downside gamma increases way more than upside gamma. Into Friday however, the upside “lets” the momentum to go squeeze up.

Net vega is very similar to the net gamma exposure Customers are net buying vol around the centroid, and selling downside vol and upside vol. Net veta is positive at ATM and negative upside and downside. So this creates a passive vol selling flow, while the upside has less and less stabilizational effect into Friday. Downside, similar to gamma keeps its supportive nature. Net vomma is negative around the centroid over the week, exagerating IV momentums, with very slight asymmetry towards “vol down” moves (like if market would “hedge” vol selling at ATM-to-centroid range, that would be however trappy into Tuesday and Friday). And it is net positive both to the upside and to the downside, with asymmetry towards “vol up” moves. And also, similarly to zomma, there is more vomma sold to the downside that to the upside. So, market is heavily selling the tails, especially left-tail. This is supportive. Net vanna is negative to the downside, but locally positive around the centroid. Low impact. This convexity is steepened by vomma, and also by zomma “in the shadow” through compressing downside and upside returns. These all tell me, that market doesn’t expect the structure to be broken.

Fixed strike skewShows bid into Wednesday mostly. It increased by 37.60% at the tails, mostly due to tail put bid, and 32.47% at the 22-deltas, mostly due to heavy call selling. Kurtosis is up by 103.10%

The key here is that skews and convexity tells market expects move towards the tails, mostly towards downside, but the positioning tells market doesn’t fear the tail moves. It doesn’t expect the structure to be broken.
