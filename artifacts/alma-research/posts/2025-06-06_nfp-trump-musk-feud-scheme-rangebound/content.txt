NFP, Trump-Musk feud, Scheme, Rangebound + Intraday post (06/June)

Fed minutes at their May meeting warned of “risks that inflation could prove more persistent than expected and that unemployment could rise”.NFP is today. A weaker‐than‐expected NFP reading signals a slowing labor market and broadening economic softening. If job gains decline materially, it suggests firms are hiring less, which raises recession risks. If NFP shows job gains < 125k, recession risk is up sharply.

SPX/LTY beta is negative in both window. Slower growth is interpreted as making Fed easing more likely. That pushes bond yields down and stocks up—reinforcing the negative beta (“good news are bad news”). Not a big euphoric happiness tho, but moderate reaction. CPI next week is pivotal along with NFP.

Always wait for the volatility to settle before take any trades on data release days. Never rush, and never trade without a conception!

Trump - Musk feud and its implicationsIn short, Trump publicly labeled Musk “crazy” and threatened to cut off Musk’s SpaceX/Starlink contracts; Musk fired back, forecasting a recession from Trump’s tariffs. Yesterday’s TSLA drop triggered VIX gamma squeeze, and dragged NDX and SPX down due to its weight. If 25–50 % tariffs on China/EU imports starting July 9, 2025 would pass, that would cause escalation, TSLA would sell off and drag broader indices down—re-amplifying volatility.The main implication is that the recent supportive Trump flow bailed the market out (tariff threats → volatility spike → Trump retreats → equities rebound) is now gone with the feud.

What does it imply? Well, even tho I see a bit lower odds of a crash, a controlled pullback (up to –20% in a recession scenario) remains possible if NFP disappoints and CPI stays elevated. Trump won’t be that willing to reverse tariffs as quickly as before.Tariffs announced in early April 2025 will fully filter through to consumer prices by July–August 2025, adding roughly 0.2 pp to core PCE over that period. Businesses will exhaust pre‐tariff inventories and begin passing higher import costs onto end‐prices. I see repositioning into July-August window. Despite aggressive vol selling into June, traders are increasingly concerned of a vol squeeze for that period (similar to 2018 Oct). Then higher odds for another 25 bp cut in Sept.
