In this article, I will outline the key elements of the geopolitical chess game to provide context for current events and market movements. The past year in the markets was all about preparing for Trump’s victory, as I noted in February. Now we are going to examine what this year’s story is.
The information comes from friends working in intelligence agencies and Middle Eastern insiders.
Everything is going to change…

Culture, religion
The soul of every society and nation is its culture. This was true in ancient times, and it remains true in the modern era—whether that culture is based on religion, philosophy, sociological structures, or even a pseudo-culture.
Yet, throughout history, the underlying interests have always remained the same: natural resources, arable land, water, and security. And that is exactly what it’s about today.
Loyalty and alliances between nations and their leaders are formed on cultural and religious foundations. The more common ground they share, the stronger the bond. And the more value a nation can bring to the negotiating table—along with effective leverage (such as military power or economic influence)—the stronger its bargaining position becomes.
These are fundamental aspects to understand.

The enemy: China
As liberalism in the Western world has expanded to extremes in certain areas, it has lost its value, leading to a decline in cultural strength. Meanwhile, across a significant portion of the world, there has been a natural resurgence in the demand for conservative order—a claim supported by statistical trends.
The rise in religious values, national capitalism, and national sovereignty is what is bringing right-wing or even far-right leaders to power in the West.

This process can be observed in both the United States and the European Union, though it is perhaps most striking in the U.S.
A Christian nationalist movement is emerging in the West, where many leaders see it as a form of ideological renewal. The Christian nationalist and Jewish groups backing Trump are known to maintain close ties with Russian Orthodoxy, viewing it as Christianity’s last European guardian angel.
Russia has deliberately left communist ideology behind and rebuilt itself on Christian nationalist foundations. This serves not only to provide a unified cultural base for its own people but also to facilitate closer ties with Europe and strengthen its relationships in the Middle East.

Both Putin, Trump, and the Arab states share a common interest in weakening China. This is primarily due to ideological differences and their political implications, as well as U.S. economic interests, since China has grown too powerful in their eyes and is becoming increasingly uncontrollable.
Until now, the Arabs communicated with China exclusively through Putin, never directly, because Putin understands the communist system and knows how to negotiate with them. It will be the same in Trump’s case as well.
The new, yet unofficial alliance is based on mutual necessity, as each member needs the others to achieve its strategic goals.
Trump’s conservative and religious rhetoric allows him to align with Putin and the Arab world.
The plan is to apply economic pressure on China by gradually reducing and eventually excluding its economic leverage. The tariffs are just the beginning, intended to force China to the negotiating table. The ‘alliance’ is working on: 1) building new logistics routes via the Northern Sea Route, which is under Russian influence, 2) technological cooperation to support mutual development and growth in efficiency, 3) exploring and developing untapped gas fields, such as the Shtokman field, to secure cheaper gas supplies for themselves and for export to Europe.
When these agreements are finalized, the Chinese economy will feel the impact.
The Trump - Putin meeting is planned to set early April, right after Ramadan.

The deal
The alliance needs strong U.S. economy. They will help to push inflation lower—even if it’s just on paper—and oil prices lower. The plan is to apply U.S. technology to extract or process oil more cheaply through Russia’s energy assets. However, the cost of technology must be significantly lower, so that Arab investors (and the funds they control) find it an attractive deal.
After buying tech at lower costs, funds can be allocated into debt instruments and bonds. So they can spread the cost of these massive projects, and reduce upfront capital burdens, basically leveraging the tech investments through debt and bonds.

Market decay and risk-on
That is why, in the Q2–Q3 period, the market will be artificially moderated—this time not by a “Fed call” as opposed to a “Fed put,” but rather by a “Gov call,” targeting the tech sector.
I still believe that in early Q2, following a supportive March Q-OpEx, we can expect a more significant plunge (using fake reasons, such as a government shutdown); until then, we will see this cautious and slow spot decay as a way to avoid a serious recession. Trump administration is deliberately engineering a mild recession. The goal is to rebalance the economy away from heavy government spending toward the private sector, even though this means short-term pain.
They need a negative wealth effect here—something I’ve been continually warning you about—but it has to be done in moderation to avoid a domino effect that could culminate in a deflationary spiral. If falling asset prices reduce household wealth (through leverage), it will naturally lead to lower spending and a slowing economy. Such an outcome would pressure the Fed to lower interest rates and weaken the U.S. dollar sooner than expected.

Insiders use this spot decay to accumulate profits on the long side with short-term long trades—hedging their risk exposure—and to buy out their opponents from various assets.

Once the peace agreements regarding Ukraine are concluded over Ukraine’s head between Russia and the United States, and once the exact blueprint for Russian–American cooperation is finalized and comes into effect, they will begin to buy in.
According to the plan, this is expected around Q3, from which point onward a steady and continuous growth can be anticipated.

WW3?
It is crucial to emphasize that none of the leaders is interested in triggering a global war, although it is important to remember that major wars have never erupted as a series of rational decisions, but rather through irrational decisions made under extremely tense circumstances.
In the eyes of the East, NATO now exists only on paper. This is primarily due to excessive ideological divisions within Europe, which are likely to deepen amid the current geostrategic processes. Another issue lies in the globalist viewpoint, which seeks to offer fair and equitable treatment to everyone yet fails to recognize that reality is entirely different. They are too late with Ukraine.
Russia has worked patiently, employing KGB-style foreign policy manipulations to undermine European unity. If Trump becomes impatient, he could easily expose the United States to foreign interests, placing the power of the American economy in the hands of Russian Jewish groups and Arab billionaires.
A peace reached too quickly is not truly peace—this is very important. Personally, I continue to believe (as I have stated for three years) that if escalation does occur, it will originate in the Balkans under pressure from the East.

Strategy
As I mentioned before, in this slow spot-decaying environment, I still see an intraday call-side edge and a short-term short-vol strategy edge, while holding March puts to hedge against decay. We must be extremely cautious in this type of market, avoid chasing, and wait for the right moment to jump in and out with our money—as if we were never there.
We'll continue going day by day until OpEx, watching closely for sudden convex increases in the pricing of tail SPX puts and tail VIX calls.
I’m bullish into EOY.
