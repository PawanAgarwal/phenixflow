I also want to citate my Thursday post here about GLD rally…

“For portfolio managers:The current gold rally is chiefly supported by central banks + ETF demand, and (statistically) that rates lead gold, not ETF flows or the dollar. The Fed’s easing bias and upside risk to breakevens are the bullish pillars.Gold is effectively short beta to real yields, so the biggest left-tail is a real-yield backup / curve steepener—that’s what we should hedge.Gold doesn’t produce cash flow and is prone to regime swings. It’s a hedge/insurance, not a compounding engine. So, treat gold as exposure to inflation/real-rate factors rather than an “asset class.” Size it by risk budget and factor diversification, not notional. Manage the overlap with duration/ breakeven risk in the rest of the portfolio, so you don’t double up on the same macro bet.Measure your portfolio’s gold to real-rate beta: regress Δgold vs Δ10y TIPS yield and vs Δbreakeven; update rolling. Adjust rates-up hedges to the beta. TY/US put spreads (short 10y/long-bond futures via puts) sized by DV01 to your estimated gold–rates beta (mind vol risk!). Or bear-steepener options (2s10s/5s30s payer spreads) to cover a potential curve-steepening risk… GLD/XAU puts or collars (e.g., 3–6M 25Δ put financed with a 10–15Δ call sale) to protect against ETF-inflow stall/reversal. Because downside is on the table if ETF inflows stop.Or if you’re not set up to manage rate hedges, consider simpler collars or a multi-asset inflation sleeve (gold + TIPS + commodities) to diversify the bet.”

WEEKLY POSTLet’s look at the weekly positioning:

The speed convexity is negative, so we have a short fly positioning clustering around 6707.36 The speed profile is net short, because participants are pricing in a heavy right-tail to the upside. Almost 3x bigger right-tail, than left… This means, that the center of the fly (the cluster) is very supportive and becomes even more so by the time (long color = positive gamma decay).

Short fly patterns tend to be pinning around the center. The centroid of the speed profile is the 6737.68 line; this is the line where the upside shorter gamma vacuum and the downside longer gamma magnet forces are in balance, so the area of the line serves as a strong pivot. Upside pivot is at 6745.89, this is where the upside vacuum effect starts to kick in (spot fully leaves behind the downside longer-gamma gravity) towards 6814.97; this is the right tail.The downside pivot is at 6676.14, where the spot would break out of the gravitational field of the center of the fly, and step into the left tail down towards 6514.36But again: the positioning is right-tail heavy.

The profile is short veta and short vomma. Short veta means, that vega decay is negative, indicating vega buyback towards Wednesday expo. While short vomma can exagerate any vol moves in any direction. These two together means, that we have a vol risk. Again: the pattern is short fly with net negative speed. Vanna is also net negative.However, below the center of the fly (also below the centroid of the speed profile), the vanna profile shifts locally towards long vanna. This means, that overall, any vol up move would support spot up too, if the spot is strong above the upside pivot. Volatility would basically ‘turn on’ the upside vacuum.However, towards the center of the fly (below the centroid) it mitigates the supportive center gamma effect (short zomma), and opens the gate of the left-tail for the spot.
