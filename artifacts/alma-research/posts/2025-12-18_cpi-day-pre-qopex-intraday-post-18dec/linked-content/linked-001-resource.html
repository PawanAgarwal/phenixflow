Liquidity
Market is just trading at fair value and it is very carefully managed so that the weekly avg returns does not exceed too much the monthly ones.
Statistically the first half of December is usually flat-to-negative in terms of performance, while the second half it steps into a steep positive phase.
We can see problems in the economy and growing uncertainity about valuation in the tech sector, with rotation into value assets, but I don’t expect it to have immediate impact. Mostly into late-Q1/Q2 next year with other policy shocks.
Growth scare is off the table, as market is positive that the Fed will support the economy for any cost. More like they welcome slowdown as it gives more room for future stimulus and easing cycle, just as bonds and stocks tells; bad news is good news.

Last week, on Friday the profit taking came one day later than I originally expected. But still market did not broke the structure.
The only important thing, liquidity-wise is a gap below us at 6765.89 - 6783.87, that should be retested in order to provide more stability and confidence for an EoM rally.
As long as this gap holds on the daily, the path of least resistance remains to the upside towards 7000 and even beyond.

We have VIX expo, M&QOpEx, NFP and retail sales on Tuesday, and CPI on Thursday, plus BoJ rate decision that effects carry trade.
Market expects in-line or cooler-than-expected for now, that they welcome.
However, a BoJ rate will likely affect risk sentiment, but more like after the OpEx week, and I don’t expect a too significant impact, as carry book isn’t that large however if the tone is more hawkish than expected, it can cause some trouble (but less odds, as their growth is kinda ok).

Market is at equilibrium, no disbalance into any direction.
The model says that any weekly close above 6622.49 makes it remain so. Market supports this zone heavily via vol selling, while betting on skew scalping on sentiment traps.

WEEKLY POSITIONING

Customers are selling left-tail pretty much for this opex, while upside vol is barely bid. The positioning is more about “protect the downside area, juicing the liquidity up there, rather than betting on upside squeezes”.

The centroid of the speed profile is right at the gap: 6753.47 - 6778.24
Not a coincidence! Big pivot to see if dip buyers step in or they let the market stabilize lower.

The positioning puts the downside pivot at 6686.66, with target of 6604.58
Unnecessary to say, that in this area, market can be expected to be sticky and rangebounding.

The upside pivot is at 6896.27, with target of 6962.42
The upside vacuum is very flat, that can provide a relatively stable upward move if it happens.
Let’s look at the greeks…

Greeks of the positioning
Net gamma is slightly positive, market bets on significant swings mostly below the upside pivot.
Net color is flat-to-positive but switches to negative into Friday. Telling us that market expect some very unstable liquidity to be built over the week, but more room for swings after CPI release.
Net zomma is positive, generating a negative correlation between IV and RV, this indicates sentiment-traps ahead, and vol scalping.

Net vega is negative over the week.
Net veta is negative until Wednesday, and flips to positive into Thursday and Friday. This means IV is expected to be bid until data releases, then MMs will need to sell it.
Net vomma, on Monday and Tuesday is negative until ±5% change in vol and flips to positive above. This provides resistance for vol moves. After Wednesday it flips to net negative, amplifying any vol moves.
Net vanna is flat-to-negative, decaying to zero into Friday, meaning market expects more vol upside and more liquidity downside.

This is like having a fly spread on volatility into Wednesday with MM negative ultima down, positive ultima up, and after a risk reversal with MM net flat-to-negative ultima.
(Ultima is like “speed for vol”, if vomma is the “gamma of vol”.)
This tells me, that vol traders expect volatility to rangebound into VIX expo but being overvalued, providing a good opportunity them to scalp it. And after data release, they are more like betting on a vol up move, that is expected to be undervalued. Slightly more odds for that, but given the relatively flat ultima, not too much more odds.
If we can believe to the positioning and assume that it is not complacent, then it means that the spot will squeeze up towards the upside pivot.
But for me, this also tells that the market expect that the spot will collect some “fuel” before this move to happen, with spot grinding downwards, or at least being below 6820 until data release. Simply because traders are net short the skew, and expect to scalp it.

This also means that VIX expo is suppressive for equitites if VIX is above 16.32 into expo. This is a key pivot for VIX. If breaks above, we will see vol buyback flow into Wednesday, if stays below and grinds lower, we will see vol selling flow after the expo.

So all-in-all, the market bets on that spot will find liquidity around the gap, and expects that the customers will overreact it and so they can scalp the skew. After that, they expect an inline or slightly cooler data print, and they can lock upside vol premium in.

All in all, the market does not expect the bullish structure to be broken this week.
However, post-OpEx, we will likely see some shake, it depends how smooth the positions roll into the next expiration. We will reassume, but there is a vol potential into post-OpEx Tuesday around GDP data.

For now, the vol profile says that
Monday can be expected to be net pessimistic and moderate risk taking;
Tuesday remains cautious with a slight shift towards more confident sentiment;
Into Wednesday, we can expect some more risk apetite to come back that is maybe too early, optimism is not yet stabilized;
On Thursday we can expect sentiment around datas to be more bening, but still uncertain, the “bad news is good news” sentiment may shifts here;
and into Friday we can expect some modest optimism, (“false breakout”-ish) and to be (suddenly) tempered. Not a pivot, but some longer-term concerns about valuations that cap the growth of the optimism.
So, sentiment looks tense, cautiously optimistic remaining under stress (longer-term concerns), paints more like a rangebounding market with a suppressed and thin right-tail and big fat body on the left-side in terms of distribution.

Don’t be married to it, it is just to have a basic idea.
Listen to the intraday updates, and manage probabilities!
