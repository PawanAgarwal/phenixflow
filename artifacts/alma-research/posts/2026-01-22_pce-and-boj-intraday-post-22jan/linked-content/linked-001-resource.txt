Net gamma switches from slightly positive to negative into Thursday, but it switches back to heavily positive into Friday, becoming 7.68x higher than it initially is on Tuesday. This provides room for volatility to increase, especially into Thursday. However downside short speed remains very convex, and downside gammas increase into Thursday, providing significant support. Net zomma is interesting. It is net positive, but it increases gamma into both ways of volatility. But in case of vol up, the gamma increase is much more bigger. …until Friday, because on Friday it shiftst to the exact oppositve: net negative, but gamma declines to both of volatility, but much less if vol goes down. This is a hidden synthetic circuit breaker that brakes the momentum in case of vol up, hinting that market wants to keep the downward momentum under control, while simultanously providing a flatter “ladder” to the upside. Also they speculate on an overvaluation of skew but only slightly. However, into Friday, this whole profile shifts: if vol goes up, gamma goes down but still remains positive, while if vol goes down, gamma goes down less. This shifts IV/RV relationship into more positive letting the vol trades to capitalize properly, mostly on vol down moves. However, note, that if IV increases by 8%+, they capitulate…

Net vega exposure is positive until Thursday, then it flips to negative;Net veta is negative until Thursday then it flips to heavily positive. So the base case is shorting IV until Thursday, slowly accumulating long vol bets, then short it again into Friday. Net vomma is positive, and it is very high exposure, with way more ultima on vol decline than vol up. This means that the path of the least resistance for volatility is up. Market bets with higher odds on vol up move through dealer vega buyback. However, on Thursday vega flips to negative, but vomma takes vega exposure up in any case. This also hints that market wants to heavily short volatility on Thursday into Friday. Net vanna is also interesting. It is net long into Thursday, on Thursday it starts to flip to net negative into Friday. However, on the first 3 days, delta net goes up anyway, and vega too into both direction. This is a passive pressure on the spot, but keeps the momentum in check, and customers goes against skew into Friday.
