Target and Walmart hiked inventories by 7 %/2.8% ahead of tariffs, then saw gross margins compress. 90‑day credit‑card delinquency jumped to 12.3%, while overall serious delinquencies climbed from 2% to 2.8% in Q1.Growth slowing with commodity prices down, but persistent inflation keeps real GDP growth subdued. A large June refinance of maturing debt and an unresolved debt‑ceiling fight threaten market liquidity and Treasury yields. Markets expect rate cuts only “after unemployment hits” and data turn—yet cuts too early risk reigniting inflation. 90‑day US/CN truce = supply shock, bcs pausing new tariffs doesn’t remove duties already in place; goods prices rose 0.2% m/m in April CPI. Reduced sailings and port congestion pushed container rates to multi‑year highs—even as global PMI shows only modest easing in supply‑chain pressures.Tariff‑shock effects on margins, credit, and growth can trigger sizable drawdowns (even 30%)—but only if data confirm a one‑two punch of slowdown plus sticky prices.Next week’s gonna be vol driven, likely vol down, spot up slow grind.
